## Why structure matters

Preparation for investment banking is often treated as a collection of isolated tasks. I prefer to treat it as an operating system with recurring routines.

## My weekly framework

- Technical training: valuation methods, accounting linkages, and transaction logic.
- Market review: key macro developments and sector-level movement.
- Communication reps: concise deal discussions and clear interview responses.
- Reflection: post-week review to identify weak spots.

## What this has improved

This process has improved both speed and confidence. More importantly, it has made my preparation measurable. I can track what I studied, how well I understood it, and where I need to focus next.

## Closing perspective

In finance recruiting, discipline compounds. A repeatable system produces better outcomes than random intensity.
